Animal Science Exploration Day (ASED) is for high school sophomores and juniors who are interested in Animal Science. It is day program running from 8 am to 5 pm. Students will be spit up into groups where they can meet other students and throughout the day, they will participate in hands-on activities pertaining to different disciplines of animal science—genetics, nutrition, physiology, meat science, and reproduction. These activities will include grading meat quality, constructing pedigrees, dissecting reproductive tracts, and an introduction to diet development. Following the activities participants will meet with current students and faculty to learn more about the curriculum. After, the meet-and-greet they will attend a panel with Mizzou Animal Science alumni who have followed different career paths where they can learn and ask questions about the different paths available through animal science. Following the panel, they will attend a tour of the Veterinary School and a teaching farm of their choice. Finally, we will reconvene as a group for a few culmination activities including a short quiz bowl with their group where they can win prizes.

30 students will be selected to attend the even on May 3rd at the Animal Science Research center at the University of Missouri-Columbia. Applications can be done in one sitting. Applications will be open until April 3rd. Applicants will know of the status of their application by April 14th.
